## Understanding the Core Features While Playing

### Starburst Expanding Wilds Explained

![Starburst Wild expanding across reel 3](/images/how-to-play/starburst-wild-expanded.webp)

The Starburst Wild is the rainbow-coloured, jewel-studded star. You cannot miss it — the screen lights up when it lands.

Here's exactly what happens:

- It only appears on **reels 2, 3, and 4** — never on the first or last reel
- The moment it lands, it **expands vertically to fill the entire reel**
- It locks in place and substitutes for every other symbol on the grid
- It immediately triggers a **free re-spin**
- That wild stays locked while the other four reels spin again

Why only the middle three reels? Because Starburst pays both ways. A wild anchored in the centre of the grid creates winning paths extending left and right simultaneously.

### Win Both Ways — What It Actually Means

![Starburst paylines running left to right and right to left](/images/how-to-play/starburst-win-both-ways.webp)

Most slots pay only for combinations running from the leftmost reel to the right. Starburst does something different.

In practice:
- A matching combination of three or more symbols counts as a win in **either direction**
- All 10 paylines are scanned **both ways** on every spin
- Near-misses that would be dead elsewhere often still pay here

If three orange gems land on reels 5, 4, and 3 reading right to left — that's a win. On most other slots, it's nothing.

### Re-Spins — How the Chain Works

Re-spins are the closest Starburst has to a bonus round. Triggered entirely by the expanding wild.

The chain:
1. A wild lands on reel 2, 3, or 4 → expands and locks → **one free re-spin**
2. During that re-spin, a new wild lands on a different middle reel → also expands and locks → **another free re-spin**
3. This chains up to a maximum of **three consecutive re-spins**

When two or three wilds are locked across the middle reels simultaneously, a huge portion of the grid is covered. Combined with Win Both Ways, that's where the slot's biggest base-game wins come from.

## Common Beginner Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

**Hitting Max Bet without thinking**
Jumping to R1,800 per spin before you've found your rhythm is a fast way to drain your balance. Start low, move up deliberately.

**Confusing re-spins with free spins**
Starburst has no traditional free spins bonus round. No scatter symbol, no separate bonus screen. The re-spins are triggered by the expanding wild and capped at three.

**Chasing losses with bigger bets**
Each spin is random and independent. Increasing your bet after a losing run doesn't shift the odds — it just makes each subsequent loss hurt more.

**Skipping the paytable**
The gap between the gold bar and the blue gem is significant. Not knowing that makes it hard to understand why some winning spins pay so much more than others.

**Underestimating the pace of the game**
A spin resolves in under two seconds. Set a phone timer or autoplay loss limits before you start.

**Treating the RTP as a per-session guarantee**
The 96.09% figure is a long-run average across millions of spins. In any single session, your actual return can swing considerably higher or lower.
