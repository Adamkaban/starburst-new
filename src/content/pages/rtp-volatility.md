## What RTP Actually Means for Players

RTP — Return to Player — is a percentage, and it's the single most important number attached to any slot game. It's also one of the most misunderstood.

The simplest way to think about it: **RTP tells you how much of all money wagered on a slot gets paid back to players over time.** A slot with 96% RTP theoretically returns R96 for every R100 wagered. But here's the catch — only across millions and millions of spins.

This is where players go wrong. Someone sees 96.09% and thinks: "so if I put in R500, I'll walk away with around R480." That's not how it works. RTP is calculated across a colossal sample size — billions of spins, spread across every player at every casino running the game simultaneously. In your session of 100 or 500 spins, results can look completely different from that theoretical figure.

What the number does tell you is something genuinely useful: **the higher the RTP, the smaller the casino's mathematical edge over the long run.** A 96% RTP slot gives the house a 4% edge. A 90% RTP slot gives them 10%. That gap compounds significantly over time.

## Starburst Volatility — Why Low Volatility Matters

If RTP tells you how much a slot pays back over time, volatility tells you how it pays — the rhythm and frequency of those payouts.

**Starburst is a low-volatility slot.** In practice, that means wins land more often — roughly one in every four to five spins, based on the 22.6% hit frequency — but individual wins tend to be on the smaller side. Your bankroll doesn't spike and crash. Sessions feel more even-keeled and predictable.

High-volatility slots work the opposite way. You might sit through 50 or 100 dry spins and then land something massive. More exciting in some ways, but genuinely brutal on a limited budget.
